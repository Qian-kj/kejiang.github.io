Hello! I’m Kejiang Qian, a Ph.D. student in Computer Science Research at King’s College London. I am an aspiring data scientist specializing in the intersection of Urban Science and Artificial Intelligence (AI). My passion lies in utilizing data-driven approaches to address complex urban challenges.

I believe in the power of technology to transform urban spaces into more sustainable, equitable, and efficient environments. My research is not just about algorithmic precision; it is about making a tangible impact on society and improving the quality of life in cities. I am dedicated to shaping the future of urban development through innovative, human-centered AI & blockchain technology.

Experience

Research Assistant, City Science Lab @ Shanghai - MIT Media Lab & Tongji University, Shanghai, China, Oct 2021 - Current

  • Research on blockchain technology: Worked on blockchain technology to encode regulations of communities and cities, such as decarbonized transportation policy and prosocial urban planning into smart contract to foster a transparent and decentralized urban environment.
